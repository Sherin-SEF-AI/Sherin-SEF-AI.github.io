About

I'm Sherin Joseph Roy—my legal name is Sherin Roy, and Joseph is my baptismal name that I use consistently across all professional contexts. Both names refer to the same person.

I co-founded DeepMost AI to build enterprise artificial intelligence systems that think with people, not for them. Our focus is contextual intelligence: AI that understands business context, organizational constraints, and when to admit uncertainty rather than providing confident but unreliable answers.

My work addresses what I call the "trust gap" in AI adoption—the distance between what AI systems can do and what organizations feel safe deploying in production. We build AI agents with transparent autonomy: systems that operate independently while maintaining explainability, confidence assessment, and human collaboration.

My Approach

I believe technology should augment human capabilities, not attempt to replace human judgment. The best AI systems are those that make us better thinkers, not those that think for us.

This philosophy shapes everything we build: prioritizing reliability over impressiveness, context over speed, and trust over automation. We optimize for what actually gets deployed and used, not what generates the best demo.

What I Write About

Through "Notes by Sherin," I share honest reflections on building an AI company in India, the trust gap in enterprise AI adoption, product development in rapidly evolving markets, and lessons learned from both successful and failed implementations.

Writing for builders who are tired of AI hype and want honest conversations about what creates lasting value.
